01 — Memory

She remembers you, securely, per person

Memory is what turns a chatbot into an assistant. Nicole keeps durable, per-user memory in the database — so she never makes you repeat yourself, and one person's information never leaks into another's.

Persisted to the database, not the chat window

Facts are written to a dedicated nicole2_memory table in Postgres, keyed by user. Each fact is stored once and updated in place (an UPSERT on user + key), so re-learning something overwrites rather than duplicates.

Why it matters: memory survives refreshes, restarts, and new devices — it lives in the database, not in a tab you can close.

Reloaded into context at the start of every session

When a conversation begins, Nicole loads everything she knows about that user and injects it as a memory block in her instructions. She treats it as ground truth and never asks you to repeat it.

Proactive, two-way, and correctable

She saves things on her own as she learns them — your name, your business, your goals, your preferences — and you can tell her to remember or forget anything. Corrections overwrite the old value cleanly.

Per-user and private by design

Every fact, profile field, and connected account is scoped to the logged-in user. The live voice session runs as that user, verified by their sign-in token — so Nicole only ever reads and acts on the data belonging to the person she's talking to. Connected-account tokens are encrypted at rest.

Why it matters: when many people use Nicole, each gets their own private memory and their own connected accounts — never shared, never crossed.
02 — Stability

Self-healing — drops repair themselves

Real-time voice runs over a live connection that will drop — networks blip, the provider rotates sessions. The difference between a toy and a product is whether the user ever notices. Nicole is built to recover silently.

Auto-reconnect with seamless resume

If the live connection drops, Nicole immediately reconnects — and reuses a resumption handle so the new session restores the prior conversation state. She picks up exactly where she left off; she never says "Hello" again or re-introduces herself.

Why it matters: a dropped socket becomes invisible — the conversation continues as if nothing happened.

Knows the difference between a blip and a wall

Ordinary drops are reconnected automatically. But terminal failures — a depleted API key, a quota or billing stop, an unsupported region — are detected and reported once, instead of retried forever. That prevents reconnect storms and surfaces the real cause.

Leak-safe by construction

Audio streams, transcript buffers, and timers are torn down deterministically when a session ends or a component unmounts. Nothing keeps running in the background, so memory and CPU stay flat over a long day of use.

Never reconnects mid-sentence

The system tracks whether you're currently speaking and holds any reconnect or maintenance until you've finished your thought — so recovery never cuts you off.

03 — Long sessions

Hour-long conversations that don't overflow

Long live sessions break two ways: context grows until the model overflows, and the provider's session handle expires after a couple of hours. Nicole gets ahead of both — proactively, before either becomes a failure.

Live summarization — the long-talk fix

As a conversation grows, Nicole compresses the older part into a running summary and keeps only the most recent turns verbatim, then reconnects seeded with that summary. Context stays small and bounded no matter how long you talk — nothing important is lost, and the model never overflows.

2
TriggerPast ~40 turns or ~12k tokens, summarization kicks in (only when you're not mid-sentence).
3
CompressOlder turns are summarized; the last 8 turns are kept word-for-word for immediate context.
4
ContinueReconnect seeded with the summary. Context is light again; the conversation flows on.

Proactive reconnect — refresh before expiry

The provider's resume handle is only good for about two hours. A watchdog checks once a minute and, once a session has been open ~100 minutes, pre-emptively reconnects on the still-fresh handle. That mints a new handle and resets the clock — comfortably under the ~110-minute safety limit — so a session can run indefinitely.

Why it matters: the session is refreshed before it can expire, never after it breaks. The 30-minute-crash class of failure is engineered out.

The throughline

Every one of these is proactive, not reactive — context is compressed before it overflows, the connection is refreshed before it expires, and drops are healed before you notice. The goal is simple: Nicole stays up, stays herself, and stays yours, for as long as you want to talk.

04 — Learning from the best

What we're taking from the top assistants

We studied the leading voice and AI assistants to define what "competition-level" actually means — then adopted the habits that separate a real assistant from a chatbot. Here's what we borrowed, and from whom.

ChatGPT Voicenatural spoken AI
Talk like a person, act in one breath. Fluid, interruptible conversation and concise result read-backs — Nicole reports what she did in a sentence ("Sent to Priya."), never re-reading the whole payload.
Google Geminiassistant + daily brief
The proactive morning brief. An opt-in daily brief that scans your calendar, unread email and due tasks, then delivers a prioritized spoken summary with a single "want me to…" next step — not an inbox dump.
Alexa+ / Sirieveryday assistant
Do things on real accounts. The table-stakes that make an assistant useful — calendar, email, tasks, messaging, music — wired as real actions on your connected services, all driven by voice.
MartinAI executive assistant
Confirm before anything irreversible. Before sending an email, posting to Slack, or booking a meeting that invites people, Nicole previews exactly what she'll do and waits for your "yes" — enforced both in her reasoning and in code, so a mis-heard word can't act on its own.
LindyAI workflow assistant
Draft-then-approve. She drafts email freely (a draft is safe — it isn't sent) and only sends on your go-ahead, so you wake up to things ready to review rather than already gone.
Perplexity / Pianswer + presence
Real-time answers, warm presence. Silent live web grounding for current facts (news, weather, prices), delivered in Nicole's own voice — accurate without sounding like a search engine.

The throughline

The common thread across all of them: an assistant isn't a chatbot — it's staff. You talk, it does the work, it confirms before anything risky, and it reports back in a breath. That's the bar we're building Nicole to meet.
